DEFINITIONS

 

Password

4.    Users must enter a string of characters, a secret word, or a passphrase to gain admission to a computer, interface, system, or network.

POLICY

5.    IS Division is responsible for managing and protecting the MBWMNet (Money Bags Wealth Management Network) through the distribution of security standards. Since network passwords are a key aspect of security and the first (and sometimes only) line of protection, they need to be designed to significantly reduce any compromise of the MBWMNet. In that spirit, a standard for creating, protecting, and using passwords on MBWS systems has been established under the following criteria:

A.    The MBWM IM/IT Network Password Policy is in effect at all times when accessing or configuring any MBWM Information System or resource that resides on it.

B.    Employees who do not adhere to this policy may be subject to disciplinary action up to and including dismissal.

General

MANDATORY MINIMUM PASSWORD REQUIREMENTS (Internal Network Users)

A.    Minimum length: 10 characters

B.    Enforced history: 24 months

C.   Maximum age: 120 days

D.   Minimum age: One day

E.    Complexity requirements to be enforced:

         User name must not be part of the password

         Password must contain at least one uppercase letter

         Password must contain at least one number

F.    Lockout duration: 15 minutes

G.   Lockout threshold: 5 attempts

H.   Reset account lockout after: 15 minutes

 

MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS

A.    Passwords are need to know and will not be revealed unless the ITSO approves it.

B.    Do not send passwords through un-encrypted mail or any other form of electronic communication considered unsecure.

C.   If you write down a password, then observe the following:

         Seal the password in an envelope.

         Keep the envelope locked up at all times.

D.   Immediately change compromised or suspected compromised passwords.

 

MANDATORY MINIMUM PASSWORD REQUIREMENTS (external Client Web Login users)

A.    Minimum length: 8 characters

B.    Enforced history: 24 months

C.   Maximum age: 120 days

D.   Minimum age: One day

E.    Complexity requirements to be enforced:

         User name must not be part of the password

         Password must contain at least one uppercase letter

         Password must contain at least one number

F.    Lockout duration: 15 minutes

G.   Lockout threshold: 25 attempts

H.   Reset account lockout after: 15 minutes

 

MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS

A.    External client passwords are need to know and will not be revealed unless the ITSO approves it.

B.    Immediately change compromised or suspected compromised passwords.

Purpose

6.    To provide minimum password requirements for access to MBWM networked resources, stand-alone and networked systems, and to define the use of those passwords.

Responsibility

CISO

7.    Enforce the password policy rules and guidelines set out in this document for all MBWM information systems through the direction of System Administrators.

ITSO

8.    Perform periodic or random password testing to ensure MBWM IM/IT Network Password Policy compliancy. In the event an investigated account is accessed, ITSO must advise Senior Operation Manager to force a password reset on the accessed account and advise user(s).

9.    Review the MBWM IM/IT Network Password Policy periodically in order to encourage the protection of information systems and services.

Guiding Principles

10.  For optimal password creation MBWM users are advised to take note of sections 11 through 13.

11.  Poor or weak passwords have the following characteristics:

A.    Password is found in a dictionary.

B.    Password is a common usage word such as:

         The name of family, pets, friends, co-workers, fantasy characters, etc.

         Computer terms and names, commands, sites, companies, hardware, software.

         Birthdays and other personal information such as addresses and phone numbers.

         Word or number patterns similar to aaabbb, 123321, etc.

         Any of the above spelled backwards.

         Any of the above preceded or followed by a digit.

12.  Strong passwords have the following characteristics:

A.    Contain both uppercase and lowercase characters.

B.    Have digits and punctuation characters as well as letters.

C.   Are at least 12 characters long.

D.   Do not contain a word found in a dictionary.

E.    Are not based on personal information.

13.  Password protection:

A.    Do not use the "Remember Password" feature of any application program such as Internet Explorer or your email program.

B.    Do not use your network password on an account over the internet that does not have a secure login. A login is secure when the URL begins with https:// rather than http://.

C.   Protect your password like a bank PIN when entering it in the presence of other users.

D.   Do not disclose your username or password to others. If someone, even your superior asks for your username and password, refer them to this document and/or have them contact the ITSO.

E.    Contact the ITSO if you feel your password has been compromised.

Procedures

ALL NETWORK USERS

14.  All users accessing any MBWM system, service or asset will adhere to this password policy as defined in order to protect the security and integrity of business services, information, and network infrastructure. All users are responsible for reporting violations to the MBWM.
