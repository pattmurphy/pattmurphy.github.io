ANNEX A

 

1. Information Reliability

 

1a. ���� Statement: There is no quality control process on the Internet; a considerable amount of information is outdated and inaccurate, and in some instances deliberately misleading. Therefore, all information taken off the Internet should be considered suspect until confirmed by separate information from another source.

 

1b. ���� Virus Checking: All files (databases, software object code, spreadsheets, formatted word processing package files, email attachments etc.) downloaded from non-MBWMNet sources via the Internet must be screened with virus detection software prior to being used. Whenever an external provider of the software is not trusted, downloaded software should be tested on a stand-alone production computer that has been recently backed-up.

 

1c. ����� Push Technology: Automatic updating of software or information on MBWMNet computers via background �push� Internet technology (having Internet users automatically download files to the MBWM network) is strictly prohibited unless the involved vendor�s system has first been tested and approved by the IS Division. While powerful and useful, this new technology could be used to spread viruses and cause other operational problems.

 

1d. ���� User Misrepresentation: Before employees release any internal MBWM information, enter into any contracts, or order any products from untrusted networks, the identity of the individuals and organizations contacted must be confirmed. Identify confirmation is ideally performed from digital signatures or digital certificates, but as these are not yet available, other means such as letters of credit, third-party references and telephone conversations may be used.

 

1e. ���� User Anonymity: Misrepresenting, obscuring, suppressing, or replacing a user�s identity on the Internet or any MBWM electronic communications system is strictly prohibited. The user name, electronic mail address, organizational affiliation, and related information included with messages or postings must reflect the actual originator of the messages or postings. Use of anonymous FTP logins, anonymous UUCP logins, and HTTP (web) browsing and other access methods established with the expectation that users would be anonymous are permissible.

 

2. Information Confidentiality and Privacy Expectations

 

2a. ���� Information Exchange: In keeping with the standard confidentiality practices within companies, MBWM software, documentation, and all other types of internal information may not be sold or otherwise transferred to any non-MBWM party for any purpose other than business expressively authorized by Division Heads and BComds through their delegated managers. Exchange of software and/or data between MBWM and any third party may not proceed unless a written agreement has first been signed. Such an agreement must specify the terms of the exchange, as well as the ways in which the software and/or data is to be handled and protected. Regular business practices, such as shipment of a product in response to a customer purchase order, need not involve such a specific agreement since the terms are implied.

 

2b. ���� Posting of Materials: All MBWM employees are to ensure that information stored and/or transmitted using computers, network and the Internet/Intranet is properly safeguarded. Employees should consult with the ISSO when there is any uncertainty as to which safeguarding measure should be taken.

 

2c. ����� Message Interception: Wiretapping and other types of message interception are straightforward and frequently encountered on the Internet.� Accordingly, MBWM secret, proprietary, or private information must not be sent over the Internet unless it has first been encrypted by approved methods. Unless specifically known to be in the public domain, source codes must always be encrypted before being sent over the Internet.

 

2d. ���� Security Parameters: Sensitive data such as personal information, passwords and PIN numbers, and other security parameters that can be used to gain access to information, goods, or services must not be sent over the Internet in a readable form. SSL V3.0+ and TSL V1.0+ are the only approved cryptographic protocols to be used to protect data that transmits through the Internet. Prior to transmitting sensitive data over the Internet through a web browser, users must ensure that the website security certificate is trusted and ok by verifying that there is a closed "padlock" icon within the browser (For more information, you can click the padlock icon.). All in-house or contracted application development for MBWM must encrypt passwords when stored and/or transmitted using CSEC (Communications Security Establishment Canada) or CC (Common Criteria for Information Technology Security Evaluation) for approved cryptographic algorithms. 

 

2e. ���� No Default Protection: Employees using MBWM information systems and the Internet should realize that their communications are not automatically protected from viewing by third parties. Unless encryption is used, employees should not send confidential or private information over the Internet.

 

2f.������� Data Management:� It shall be understood that Mailbox (Email) Accounts are owned by MBWM. However, since the individual user is given access in such a manner that some personal use is granted some level of privacy should be expected.� Therefore, the business only grants access to assigned security and administrative IM/IT custodians with �need-to-know� who are previously identified on an Access Control List (ACL).� The ACL can be obtained from the ITSO upon request through ITSS.

 

2g.������ Data Transfer: In some situations internal information (data) transfers are required; which may include email.� These will be approved by the individual requestor�s managerial staff in accompaniment with the expressed permission of the individual user.� Transfers require the completion of the Network Account Maintenance Form which must clearly state the requirement.� In extreme circumstances where expressed permission is unavailable, the approval of MBWM or representative will be required.

 

In cases where users with mailboxes are terminated or otherwise leave unexpectedly IS Division will not in any way fulfill a request to forward a mailboxes to another address.� Forwarding to other addresses will only be done on expressed permission by the user of the mailbox.

 

In both cases stated express permission must document approval with a form of signature.

 

����������� For former employees, request for information or legal requirements MBWM is obliged to conform to the Access to Information Act and will release information based upon an appropriate approved request through Human Resources.����

 

3. Public Representations

 

3a. ���� External Representations: Employees may indicate their affiliation with MBWM in mailing lists, chat sessions, and other offerings on the Internet. This may be done by explicitly adding certain words or it may be implied, for example, via an email address. In either case, whenever employees provide an affiliation, they must also clearly indicate that the opinions expressed are their own, or not necessarily those of MBWM. If an affiliation with MBWM is provided, political advocacy statements and product/service endorsements are also prohibited. With the exception of general marketing and customer service activities, the CIO must first clear all representations on behalf of MBWM.

 

3b. ���� Appropriate Behaviour: To avoid libel, defamation of character, and other legal problems, whenever any affiliation with MBWM is included with an Internet message or posting, "flaming" or similar written attacks are strictly prohibited. Likewise, employees must not make threats against another user or organization over the Internet. All Internet messages intended to harass, annoy, or alarm another person are similarly prohibited.

 

3c.������ Removal of Postings: Those messages sent to Internet discussion groups, electronic bulletin boards, or other public forums, which include an implied or explicit affiliation with MBWM may be removed if management deems them to be inconsistent with MBWM's business interests or policy. Messages in this category include: (a) political statements (b) religious statements (c) cursing or other foul language (d) statements viewed as harassing others based on race, creed, color, age, sex, physical disability, or sexual orientation. The Chief Information Officer (CIO), in consultation with the appropriate Division Head may quarantine/remove offensive messages as required. Individuals responsible for offending messages will be advised, and where practical, will be afforded the opportunity to amend the message, or remove it themselves.

 

3d.������ Compliance to Canada�s Anti-Spam Law (CASL):

 

����������� There is now a responsibility to clients in regards of protection from Spam originating from MBWM.� Communication from MBWM must be opted into and will comply with the requirements defined by CASL.� Creation of material can be penalized by fines up to and including criminal charges where the business and individual officers and/or directors may be held responsible.

 

4. Intellectual Property Rights

 

4a. ���� Copyrights: MBWM supports strict adherence to software vendors' license agreements. When at work, or when MBWM computing or networking resources are employed, copying of software in a manner that is not consistent with the vendor's license is strictly forbidden. Also prohibited are off-hours participation in pirate software bulletin boards and similar activities that represent a conflict of interest with MBWM work. Reproducing, forwarding, or in any other way republishing or redistributing words, graphics, or other materials without the permission of the author/owner is also prohibited. Employees should assume that all materials on the Internet are copyrighted unless specific notice states otherwise.

 

4b.������ Publicly-Writable Directories: All publicly-writable directories on MBWM Internet-connected computers will be reviewed and cleared each evening. This process is necessary to prevent the anonymous exchange of information that is inconsistent with MBWM business. Examples include pirated software, purloined passwords, stolen credit card numbers, and inappropriate written or graphic material, such as pornography.

 

5. Access Control

 

5a. ���� User Authentication: Users are not permitted to connect to the MBWM internal network via the Internet, unless appropriate security controls have been installed to establish a real-time connection using VPN. This authentication process is achieved via a dynamic password system approved by the IM/IT Department. Designated "public" systems (anonymous FTP, web surfing, etc.) do not need user authentication processes because anonymous interactions are expected.

 

5b. ���� Internet Service Providers: With the exception of telecommuters and mobile computer users, employees must not use Internet Service Provider (ISP) accounts and dial-up lines to access the Internet with MBWM computers.� Instead, all Internet activity must pass through MBWM firewalls so that access controls and related security mechanisms can be applied.

 

5c. ����� Establishing Network Connections: Unless the prior approval of the Director IM/IT Operations has been obtained, employees may not establish Internet or other external network connections that could allow non-MBWM users to gain access to MBWM systems and information. These connections include the establishment of multi-computer file systems (like Sun's NIS), Internet web pages, FTP servers, and the like.

 

5d. ���� Establishing New Business Channels: Employees are prohibited from using new or existing Internet connections to establish new business channels, unless approved by MBWM Legal Counsel. These channels include electronic data interchange (EDI) arrangements, electronic malls with on-line shopping, online database services, etc.

 

5e. ���� Web Content Management:� IS Division activity manages web content internally and/or externally available to the end-user.� Employees using MBWMNet computers who discover they have connected to a website that contains sexually explicit, racist, violent, or other potentially offensive material must immediately disconnect from that site and identify the incident to the ITSO through ITSS (Information Technology Support Services).� In addition, the address shall be indentified so that it may be blocked to future use. Note that the ability to connect with a specific website does not in itself imply that users of MBWMNet may be permitted to visit that site.

 

Management is preformed in the following two ways:

 

1.    Active Management of External Content.

 

a.    Though the employment of a web filtering system, information considered to be inappropriate for business use is blocked based on predefined Access Control Lists (ACL).� Content that has been blocked may be unfiltered under certain circumstances.� In addition to the ability to block content, MBWM also has the ability to report on usage history where situations indicate a requirement.� In these events the business must justify the investigative action and action shall be warranted by the MBWM.��

 

2.    Management of Approved Internal Content.

 

a.    In conjunction with the Strategic Communications cell, internal content is developed and approved for end-user access based on certain predefined criteria supporting MBWM initiatives.

 

 

6. Personal Use

 

6a. ���� Personal Use: Employees are encouraged to explore the Internet, but if this exploration is for personal purposes, it must be done on personal time and not work time. Likewise, games, news groups, and other non-business activities must be performed on personal time and not work time. Use of MBWM computing resources for these personal purposes is permissible provided the incremental cost of the usage is negligible, and provided no MBWM business activity is pre-empted by the personal use. Employees must not use the Internet or other internal information systems in such a way that the productivity of other employees is eroded; examples include chain letters and broadcast charitable solicitations, private business.

 

7. Reporting Security Problems

 

7a. ���� Notification Process: The ITSO must be notified immediately through ITSS if (a) sensitive piece of MBWM information is lost or disclosed to unauthorized parties, is suspected of being lost or disclosed to unauthorized parties, (b) any unauthorized use of MBWM information systems has taken place or is suspected of taking place, (c) passwords or other system access control mechanisms are lost, stolen, or disclosed, or are suspected of being lost, stolen, or disclosed, (d) there are unusual system behaviours such as missing files, frequent system crashes and misrouted messages that may indicate a computer virus infection or similar security problem. The specifics of security problems should not be discussed widely but should instead be shared on a need-to-know basis.

 

7b. ���� False Security Reports: The Internet has been plagued with hoaxes alleging various security problems. Many of these hoaxes take the form of chain letters that request that the receiving party send the message to other people. Employees in receipt of information about system vulnerabilities should forward it to ITSS, who will then determine what, if any, action is appropriate. Employees must not personally redistribute system vulnerability information.� Note that the only official IT security alerting mechanism is the IT Security Office through the ITSS dissemination system of choice.

 

7c. ����� Testing Controls: Employees must not "test the doors" (probe) of security mechanisms at either MBWM or other Internet sites, nor may they use tools to crack information security because alarms are triggered and resources are needlessly spent tracking the activity.�

 

 

8. Disciplinary Action

Employees violating any of the above noted requirements are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including discharge.

RULES FOR MBWMNET USE

 

1.       Downloading malicious files from the Internet or accepting the same from email messages onto MBWM computers is strictly prohibited, unless authorized by Director IM/IT Operations through the communication with Information Technology Service Desk. Such authorization shall normally only be granted for research purposes and the files shall be subject to testing and approval by IS Division prior to download. Updating/upgrading authorized downloaded files shall also be subject to testing and approval by the IS Division prior to update/upgrade (for example, LiveMeeting is considered pre-authorized).

 

2.       MBWM software, documents such as protected information for internal use, and all other types of internal documents are the property of MBWM. Copying or transferring these materials to parties not employed by MBWM without authorization is strictly prohibited. The sale of the same is prohibited.

 

3.       Those with access to MBWM systems that contain PROTECTED information must ensure that access to such information is safeguarded from unauthorized parties. Information contained within those systems may only be accessed and used for business purposes and must not be shared with external parties.

 

4.       Sharing personal user account information including user names and passwords is strictly prohibited.

 

5.       Any suspected unauthorized use of MBWMNet systems, lost or stolen passwords and potential viruses must be reported immediately to Information Technology Support Services (ITSS).

 

6.       The distribution of messages containing statements viewed as profanity, harassment and discriminatory (including but not limited to sexually explicit, sexist or racist statements) are strictly prohibited.

 

7.       Browsing websites containing discriminatory, sexually explicit, racist, violent, or other material deemed offensive is strictly prohibited. If such a site is located accidentally, close the page and immediately notify ITSS.

 

8.       Redistribution of chain mail, jokes, and notices of computer viruses from any source is strictly prohibited.

 

 

9.       Social media use is permitted however use must be within the guidelines of the MBWM Social Media Policy (available through the Strategic Communications cell).

 

10.    Attempts to bypass system security controls such as firewalls and passwords are strictly prohibited.

 

11.    MBWM computers may be used for personal use during non-business hours such as breaks, lunch, at the beginning or end of the work day, and outside working hours for Internet browsing and email. When using MBWM computers for personal use, users must still comply with all policies disseminated.

 

12.    Anyone with access to MBWMNet computers and software must adhere to instructions/directions received from IS Division regarding system access, system capacity, viruses, and anti-virus software upgrades. Under no circumstances are individual PC firewalls or anti-virus applications to be turned off.

 

13.    The MBWM monitors and collects activity on the MBWMNet without explanation or notification to the employee.� The aggregation of this data has potential to define effectiveness and may be used in conjunction with Human Resource practices where required.
